A nutrient is a component in feeds that an animal uses to survive and grow, and is found in varying amounts in all feedstuffs. The macronutrients - the first 56 in our list - provide the bulk of an animal's metabolic needs, while the rest, the micronutrients, provide the necessary cofactors for metabolism to be carried out. Energy, although strictly speaking not a nutrient, is derived and provided to the animal from the protein, fat, carbohydrate and amino acid components of the macronutrients.
